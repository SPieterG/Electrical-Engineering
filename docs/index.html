ยง2 Motion in a straight line:
constant acceleration: $$\vec{v}=\lim_{\Delta t\to\infty} \frac{\Delta{x}}{\Delta{t}}=\frac{dx}{dt}$$ $$\vec{a}=\lim_{\Delta t\to\infty} \frac{\Delta{v}}{\Delta{t}}=\frac{dv}{dt}$$ $$\vec{v}=v_0+at$$ $$x=x_0+\frac{1}{2}(v_o+v)t$$ $$x=x_0+v_ot+\frac{1}{2}at^2$$ $$v^2=v_0^2 +2a(x-x_0)$$

no constant acceleration: $$v(t)=\int_{0}^{t}a(t)dt$$ $$x(t)=\int_{0}^{t}v(t)dt$$

ยง3 Motion in two and three dimensions:

Vectors addition is commutive: $\vec{A}+\vec{B} = \vec{B}+\vec{A}$ vector addition is also associative: $$(\vec{A}+\vec{B})+\vec{C} = \vec{A}+(\vec{B}+\vec{C})$$ $$\vec{A}+\vec{B}\rightarrow(A_x\hat{i}+A_y\hat{j})+(B_x\hat{i}+B_y\hat{j})=(A_x+B_x)\hat{i}+(A_y+B_y)\hat{j}$$

uniform circular motion: $$E_k = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$
