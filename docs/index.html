§2 Motion in a straight line:
constant acceleration: $$\vec{v}=\lim_{\Delta t\to\infty} \frac{\Delta{x}}{\Delta{t}}=\frac{dx}{dt}$$ $$\vec{a}=\lim_{\Delta t\to\infty} \frac{\Delta{v}}{\Delta{t}}=\frac{dv}{dt}$$ $$\vec{v}=v_0+at$$ $$x=x_0+\frac{1}{2}(v_o+v)t$$ $$x=x_0+v_ot+\frac{1}{2}at^2$$ $$v^2=v_0^2 +2a(x-x_0)$$

no constant acceleration: $$v(t)=\int_{0}^{t}a(t)dt$$ $$x(t)=\int_{0}^{t}v(t)dt$$

§3 Motion in two and three dimensions:

Vectors addition is commutive: $\vec{A}+\vec{B} = \vec{B}+\vec{A}$ vector addition is also associative: $(\vec{A}+\vec{B})+\vec{C} = \vec{A}+(\vec{B}+\vec{C})$ $$\vec{A}+\vec{B}\rightarrow(A_x\hat{i}+A_y\hat{j})+(B_x\hat{i}+B_y\hat{j})=(A_x+B_x)\hat{i}+(A_y+B_y)\hat{j}$$

uniform circular motion: $$\vec{a}=\frac{d\vec{v}}{dt}$$ $$\vec{v}=\frac{d\vec{r}}{dt}$$ $$A=\sqrt{A_x^2+A_y^2}$$ $$A_x= Acos(\theta)$$ $$A_y=Asin(\theta)$$ $$\theta=tan^{-1}(\frac{A_y}{A_x})$$

§4 Force and motion:
$$\vec{p}=m\vec{v}$$ $$\vec{F}_{net}=\frac{d\vec{p}}{dt}=\frac{d(m\vec{v})}{dt}=m\frac{d\vec{v}}{dt}=m\vec{a}$$ $$\vec{w}=m\vec{g}$$ $$F_s=-kx$$

§5 Using Newron's Laws:
$F_{net}=ma=\frac{mv^2}{r}$ (uniform circular motion) $$f_k=\mu_kFn$$

§6 Energy, Work and Power:
$$w=F_x\Delta{x}=\vec{F}\cdot\Delta\vec{r}$$ The scalar product of any two vectors $\vec{A}$ and $\vec{B}$ is defined as $\vec{A}\cdot\vec{B}=ABcos(\theta)$ where A and B are the magnitudes of the vecotrs and $\theta$ is the angel between them. $$W=\int_{x_1}^{x_2}F(x)dx$$ $$W=\int_{0}^{x}F(x)dx=\int_{0}^{x}kx\cdot{dx}=\frac{1}{2}[kx^2]_{0}^{x}=\frac{1}{2}kx^2$$ $$E_k = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$
